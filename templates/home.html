"Preprocessed Text" marks a crucial phase in the journey towards fake news detection. Here, the raw information undergoes meticulous refinement, involving techniques such as tokenization, stemming, and the removal of stop words. This process enhances the text's clarity, simplifies its structure, and prepares it for effective analysis by machine learning algorithms. The goal is to extract meaningful features and patterns while minimizing noise, ensuring a more accurate and reliable classification.

"Transform & Predict" phase represents the convergence of refined text and advanced machine learning. The preprocessed text undergoes a conversion into a numerical format, often a vector, before being fed into a trained model. This model, having learned from vast datasets, applies its acquired knowledge to classify the input text as genuine or fake. The predictive aspect of this process, driven by the model's ability to discern patterns, contributes significantly to the overarching goal of identifying and mitigating the impact of misinformation in today's information-rich environment.
